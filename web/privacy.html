Privacy Policy

For the "Fuck Clairo" album downloader web app.

This is a small, invite-only tool built for a private group of friends - it is not a public service, and access is limited to people the site owner has shared the URL with directly.

What data we access

If you choose to log in with your Spotify account, the app requests read-only access to:

  • Your saved albums and liked songs (user-library-read)
  • Your playlists, including private and collaborative ones (playlist-read-private, playlist-read-collaborative)
  • Your basic profile info (display name and profile picture), fetched once at login

We never request write access, and nothing is ever added, removed, or changed in your Spotify account or library.

Why

This data is used for exactly one feature: scanning your library and playlists to find tracks that are unavailable for streaming in a market you choose, so you can see what's region-locked and download it separately. The results are shown only to you.

How it's stored

Your login session is kept server-side and expires automatically after a few hours. To make repeat scans faster, a cache of your library contents (album/track IDs, not audio or personal messages) is stored on the server, kept separate per person, and never shown to or shared with any other user of the site.

Third parties

Your Spotify data is never sold, shared, or sent to any third party. The app also looks up track metadata from MusicBrainz (a free, public music database) as a fallback when an album isn't on Spotify - no personal or account data is sent there, only artist/album names.

Deleting your data

Logging out ends your session immediately. To have your cached library data removed from the server entirely, contact the site owner directly.
